\underline{\textbf{Selected consulting experience}}
	
	\begin{itemize}
		\item In the major antitrust litigation, AMD v. Intel, supported plaintiff’s testifying expert by performing econometric analysis to determine the impact of alleged conduct on market shares and prices using a treatment effect approach.
\item Supported multiple testifying experts on behalf of direct and indirect plaintiffs in In re Dynamic Random Access Memory (DRAM) Antitrust Litigation. Serving as the data modeling manager responsible for developing the damages model used to compute purchaser overcharges and damages. Other responsibilities include analyzing and standardizing defendant sale and client purchase data, understanding the DRAM industry, and collecting information on factors that affect DRAM pricing. Also supporting testifying experts on preparation of evidence and testimony for depositions and rebuttal reports.
\item Performed economic and statistical analysis regarding liability and damages on behalf of a large coalition of direct-action plaintiffs in In re Linerboard Antitrust Litigation. Developed an econometric model to calculate production downtime to support the analysis and opinions of the liability expert. Worked with the econometrics team to support settlement and mediation negotiations, discovery, and critique of opposing expert analyses.
\item Supported the causation and damages expert on behalf of the unsecured creditors committee in Enron Corp and Enron North America Corp v. Citigroup Inc. Provided econometric and statistics support for liability analysis and damage apportionment. Used historical trading data to develop a probabilistic model to estimate the date by which Enron would have lost its investment grade status had the company reported its transactions with the bank defendants in a manner reflective of its true economic substance. 
\item Supported analysis presented to the FTC concerning the antitrust implications of The Great Atlantic & Pacific Tea Company’s (A&P) proposed acquisition of Pathmark Stores, Inc. on behalf of the acquiring party. Developed and implemented econometric analysis to evaluate the impact of the entry and exit of over 200 supermarkets, grocery stores, mass merchandisers, clubs, and other retailers of food on prices, margins, and sales. Studied detailed product-level sales information to construct price indices that controlled for possible changes in product mix in order to isolate price effects.
\item Provided statistical and economic analysis on behalf of a major financial institution with multiple prime and subprime mortgage business units. Developed statistical models to detect pricing disparities between protected and unprotected classes. Evaluated mortgage pricing strategies to examine the relationship between pricing and underlying credit risk, and developed alternate risk-adjusted pricing algorithms to reduce pricing disparities.

	\end{itemize}
